Ceviche:

  • Time: 4.5 hrs.
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Skinless snapper fish 1 lb.
Fresh lime juice 1.5 cups
Medium white onion, cubed 1 onion, 1/2 in. cubes
Medium-large tomato, cubed 2 tomatoes, 1/2 in. cubes
Jalapeños, stemmed, seeded, chopped 1 to 2, choose wisely :)
Cilantro, chopped 1/3 cup
Manzanillo olives, pitted 1/3 cup
Extra-virgin olive oil 1 to 2 tbsp
Salt To taste :)
Fresh orange juice 3 tbsp
Ripe avocado, peeled, pitted , and diced 1 large, or 2 small
Tortilla chips, for serving To taste :)

Step 1: In a large 1 1/2 quart bowl, preferably stainless steel, mix together the fish, lime juice , and onion . Cover the bowl and refrigerate the fish for about 4 hours . The acid from the lime juice will sufficiently "cook" and disinfect the fish, rendering it edible. The longer you let the fish sit, the more tangier the taste becomes. 4 hours is minimal. Once finished, drain in a colander.

Step 2: In a larger bowl, mix together the tomatoes, jalapeños, cilantro, olives and olive oil. Once finished, stir in the fish, and season with salt, to taste. Mix in the orange juice. If not serving immediately, refrigerate. Before serving, gently mix in the avocados. Enjoy!

Mushroom Risotto:

  • Time: 1.0 hr.
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Low sodium vegetable broth 8 cups
Extra-virgin olive oil 1 tbsp
Onion, finely chopped 1 onion
Butter, divided 2 tbsp
Garlic cloves, minced 2 cloves
Button mushrooms, sliced 1 lb.
Bay leaf 1 leaf
Thyme sprigs, leaves removed 4 sprigs
Kosher salt To taste :)
Black pepper, ground To taste :)
Arborio rice 2 cups
White wine 1/2 cup
Parmesan cheese, grated 1 cup
Frozen peas, thawed 3/4 cup
Fresh parsley, chopped 2 tbsp

Step 1: Bring the vegetable broth to a simmer in a medium pot, over medium heat. Once finished, reduce flame to low.

Step 2: In a larger pot, heat the olive oil. Add the onion and caramelize until translucent; about 5 minutes. Add half the butter. Add the garlic, mushrooms, bay leaf, and thyme. Cook for about 4 minutes, until the mushrooms are browner and softer. Then season with the salt and pepper. Once finished, transfer the mixture to another pot.

Step 3: Melt the rest of the butter in the now empty pot. Add the arboria rice, stirring constantly and quickly, until the rice smells lightly toasted; about 2 minutes. Pour in the white wine, and stir until it is mostly absorbed.

Step 4: Add about 1 cup of hot broth to the rice, stirring constantly. Stir until the rice has absorbed all the broth. Repeat the process until the rice is creamy and moist. Remember, add broth in increments of 1 cup at a time, and you might not need to use all of it.

Step 5: Add the rest of the mixture to the rice. Stir in parmesan and peas, then garnish with the parsley. Serve warm. Enjoy!

Lemon Meringue Pie:

  • Time: 1.75 hrs.
  • Serves: 8

Important: Have a premade pie crust ready; it can be store bought, or homemade.

For the Lemon Filling:

Ingredients Quantity
Large egg yolks 5 yolks
Cornstarch 6 tbsp
Sugar 1 1/3 cups
Salt 1/4 tsp
Water 1 1/2 cups
Lemon juice 1/2 cups
Lemon zest 2 tsp
Butter 2 tbsp

For the Meringue:

Ingredients Quantity
Cornstarch 1 tbsp
Cold Water 1/3 cup
Cream of Tartar 1/4 tsp
White granulated sugar 1/2 cup, plus 2 tbsp
Large egg whites, room temperature 5 egg whites
Vanilla extract 1/2 tsp

Step 1: Cover the pie crust with foil, and pre-bake it for 20 minutes. Follow the temperature instructions if it is store-bought. If it is homemade, bake at 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Remove foil, poke holes at the bottom of the crust, then bake more for 15 minutes. While that is happening...

Step 2: Whisk the egg yolks in a bowl. Make the filling: Set aside. In a medium saucepan, add cornstarch, sugar, salt, and water. Bring to a boil, and let it simmer until mixture is thick. When done, remove from heat. Take spoonfulls of the cornstarch mix and whisk it into the egg yolks. Continue, adding cornstarch mix in increments of a spoonfull, until half of the cornstarch mix is used. Add egg yolk mix to the cornstarch, and boil for 3 to 4 minutes. Remove from heat. Stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter.

Step 3: Preheat the oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit. Next, make the meringue: In a small saucepan, mix cornstarch and cold water to dissolve the cornstarch. Heat until mix thickens. Set aside. Whisk together sugar and cream of tartar. Then, set aside. Place egg whites and vanilla extract in your food mixer bowl. Start beating the mix, slowly increasing the speed. When egg whites are frothy, mix in the sugar and cream of tartar mix slowly. Next, add the cornstarch water mix, slowly. Mix all until the meringue forms smooth peaks.

Step 4: Heat the lemon filling once more, until it's boiling. Scoop the mix into the pre-baked pie crust, spreading it evenly. Use a rubber spatula to spread the meringue around the edge of the pie, and then in the center if wanted. Form patterns in the meringue, if wanted. Bake the whole pie for 20 minutes, at 325 degrees Fahrenheit. Let pie cool before serving. Enjoy!
